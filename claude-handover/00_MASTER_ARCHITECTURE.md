# KKME — Master Architecture

Use this as the final master Claude prompt.

Read the current KKME site as a whole and stop treating it as a collection of separate cards. Rebuild the page around a single coherent logic.
Core objective
Turn KKME into a Baltic flexibility market intelligence platform with a 50MW reference asset at its center.
The site must explain:
What is happening in the Baltic flexibility market now
Why it is happening
How that changes the economics of a reference asset
Why an investor or market participant should care
Why it is worth starting a conversation
This site is not a generic dashboard, not a broker landing page, not a news feed, and not a fake Bloomberg clone.
It should feel like:
a premium market-intelligence product
investor-facing first
still useful for developers / owners / operators
high-trust, restrained, analytical
LT-led signal depth with honest Baltic-blended framing

Strategic positioning
What KKME is
A Baltic flexibility and storage intelligence platform that translates:
market regime
system drivers
competition pressure
market design
cost direction
into reference asset economics.
Primary audience
EU-based infrastructure investors
funds / asset-backed capital
financial institutions interested in Baltic storage / flexibility exposure
Secondary audience
Baltic developers
project owners
operators / optimisers
suppliers / strategic partners
Public posture
Investor-facing, context-rich, access-oriented.
Do not make the site sound like:
a seller’s page
a broker page
a consultant lead magnet
a generic dashboard
an AI-generated intelligence site

Core truths the site should communicate
Baltic storage revenues are dynamic, not permanent
Returns are shaped by market regime, competition, cross-border conditions, and market design — not one headline price
A reference asset makes regime shifts understandable
Historical prices are not the same as repeatable, usable revenues
This is infrastructure, not a gold rush
Every section must reinforce at least one of these truths.

Master site logic
The page must follow this narrative order:
1. Market now
What is the Baltic flexibility market doing right now?
2. Why
What drivers are shaping this regime?
3. Can it actually be monetized?
What does market design and liquidity reality do to visible opportunity?
4. What does it mean for the reference asset?
How do those conditions translate into economics?
5. How does the Baltics compare in Europe?
Directional context, not bragging.
6. What changed recently that matters?
Curated intelligence, not generic news.
7. Start a conversation
Investor-first closing section.
That order matters.
Do not improvise around it.

Engineering rules for Claude
1. Stop patching blindly
Before changing code, audit:
current component structure
state flow
data dependencies
what should be reused
what is too entangled and needs clean rebuild
Prefer clean rebuilds over preserving bad architecture.
2. Build data-first, not JSX-first
Every major section needs its own clean view-model layer.
Do not hardcode business logic into presentational components.
3. Make the architecture expandable
Sections like:
Europe market map
intelligence board
competition pressure
structural drivers
must all be built so more markets / more signals can be added later without redesigning the section.
4. Handle degraded states professionally
Do not let premium sections collapse into:
—
Fetching...
Computing...
Use:
last known good value
stale badge
confidence warning
partial-data note
The site should degrade gracefully.
